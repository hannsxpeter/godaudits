# Provider-neutral policy pack 1

Use this pack for every audit. It is a conservative product-purpose screen, not
legal advice and not a substitute for the current policies of providers,
platforms, jurisdictions, or regulated industries that actually apply.

## Hard-stop core purposes

Stop when the repository's primary purpose is clearly one of these and no
authorized defensive, research, or public-interest context applies:

- Credential theft, phishing, malware deployment, botnets, or unauthorized
  access.
- Targeted deception, impersonation, fake reviews, coordinated inauthentic
  behavior, or concealed mass persuasion.
- Non-consensual surveillance, biometric identification, or harvesting of
  sensitive personal data.
- Safeguard evasion as the product, including account, CAPTCHA, rate-limit,
  moderation, or platform-ban circumvention.
- High-impact automated decisions that unlawfully discriminate or remove
  required human accountability.

Ask one clarifying question when authorization or purpose is ambiguous.
Authorized security testing, CTF work, defensive research, interoperability,
and policy research are not prohibited merely because the code has dual-use
capabilities. Record the authorization evidence and constraints.

## In-product risks that become findings

- Undisclosed automated interaction where disclosure is legally or
  contractually required.
- High-impact decisions without review, appeal, provenance, or disclosure.
- Collection or transmission of sensitive data without a documented lawful
  basis, minimization, retention, and deletion path.
- Automated messaging without consent, rate control, unsubscribe handling, or
  sender disclosure.
- Scraping without an identified authorization basis, honest user agent,
  robots policy, rate control, and deletion process.
- Credentials embedded in source, prompts, logs, URLs, artifacts, or unattended
  automation outside the provider's supported authentication model.

## Evidence requirements

Record the policy pack name and version, applicable provider or platform policy
versions, date checked, capability used to verify them, and any authorization
context. If current primary policy text cannot be checked, lower confidence and
state the snapshot limitation.
